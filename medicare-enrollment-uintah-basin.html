When Can You Enroll?

Initial Enrollment Period (IEP)

When you first qualify for Medicare (typically around age 65). Your IEP is the 7-month window that starts 3 months before your 65th birthday month, includes your birthday month, and continues for 3 months after.

Annual Enrollment Period (AEP)

October 15 – December 7 every year. This is when you can change your Medicare Advantage or Part D plan. Changes take effect January 1.

Special Enrollment Period (SEP)

If you qualify due to certain life events (moving, losing other coverage, etc.), you may have a Special Enrollment Period. We'll review your situation to see if you qualify.

Enrollment Questions

How long does enrollment take?

The enrollment application typically takes 10-15 minutes once you've chosen a plan. The plan comparison and consultation usually takes 30-60 minutes, depending on your questions.

What if I miss my enrollment period?

Depending on your situation, you may face late enrollment penalties or have to wait until the next enrollment period. Contact us as soon as possible to review your options.

Can I change plans after I enroll?

Yes, during the Annual Enrollment Period (October 15 – December 7) or if you qualify for a Special Enrollment Period. We'll help you review your plan each year to make sure it still meets your needs.

Is there a cost for enrollment help?

No. Our Medicare enrollment assistance is completely free to you. We're licensed and compensated by the insurance plans, so you receive expert guidance at no charge.
